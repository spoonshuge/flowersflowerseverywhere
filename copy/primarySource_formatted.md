## About Me

### Who Am I

I am an **experiential therapist** and **collaborative, relationally-oriented counselor** who seeks to support individuals within their current context. While I make no guarantees about the outcomes of therapy, there are two promises I make to all my clients:

**First Promise:** Because I am a firm believer that you can only take someone as far as you yourself have gone, I will not ask anything of you that I don't ask of myself. Therefore, every technique used in session or trainings is one that I have not only been trained in but also experienced personally.

**Second Promise:** I will not suggest anything that I do not believe will be to your benefit. With that said, I let all my clients know that you always have veto power and all suggestions are invitations.

#### My Journey

Prior to becoming a **Nationally Certified Counselor**, I obtained my psychology degree from **Hendrix College** before moving to Atlanta to attend **Emory University's Candler School of Theology** and earn my **Master of Divinity**. 

Throughout my education, I sought to ground my academic work in community-based service such as:
- Crossroads Community Ministries
- Oakhurst Baptist Church
- Genesis Family Shelter

After graduating from theology school and serving as program developer and administrator in the non-profit sector, I realized that I wanted to work more closely with individuals and chose to return to school to earn my **Master's Degree in Clinical Mental Health Counseling** at **Mercer University**. 

I then completed my training and early clinical experience at Genesis Family Shelter and Compass Consulting. These days, I enjoy working with clients, especially in intensives, and providing experiential consultation and training to those hoping to bring more action into their therapeutic practice.

### My Approach

I use **experiential modalities** to integrate humanistic, existential, and systems theories with cognitive, behavioral, and solution-focused techniques. I seek to work with clients to identify strengths, better utilize available resources, and work through challenges that pose obstacles to fulfilling life and relationships. 

Rather than providing traditional talk therapy, I integrate practices of mindfulness and experiential approaches such as:

- **Psychodrama**
- **Art Therapy**
- **Music Therapy**
- **Improvisation**
- **Sand Tray Therapy**
- **Brainspotting**
- **Play Therapy**
